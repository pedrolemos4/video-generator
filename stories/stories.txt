The old lighthouse had been dark for thirty years. Every night, fishermen would navigate by memory, cursing the broken beacon that once guided them home. One stormy evening, a young girl climbed the rusted stairs with nothing but a lantern and a stubborn heart. She couldn't fix the light. She couldn't stop the storm. But she held that lantern high in the window until every last boat found the shore. Nobody ever learned her name. But from that night on, no fisherman ever sailed without a spare light of his own.
